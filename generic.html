About
Shola Folarin

"Torture the data, and it will confess to anything." - Ronald Coase This quote guides my approach to computational nanoscience and data analysis, encapsulating the essence of my professional journey. With a Master's degree in Applied Physics from the University of South Florida, I am a dedicated explorer at the crossroads of science and data. My expertise includes harnessing Monte Carlo and Molecular Dynamics simulations, wielding Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations, and skillfully using Python and machine learning techniques (Linear Regression, SVM, CNNs, RNNs). I've led transformative projects such as: Predictive analytics for fcc-forum-pageviews Unveiling insights with Demographic_data_analyzer Medical data storytelling in Mmedical_Data_Visualizer_Project Forecasting with Sea-Level-Predictor Navigating the depths of the Titanic with Spaceship-Titanic-Project Predicting housing prices in Mexico and Buenos Aires My journey in Computational Nanoscience has been marked by groundbreaking publications: "Hidden variable in electrocaloric effect" (Published) "Enhanced piezoelectric response in nanoscale vortex structures in ferroelectrics (Submitted)" I'm not just seeking a job; I'm on a mission to make a meaningful impact. Let's connect and fuse science and data to shape our dynamic world. Together, we can uncover the secrets hidden within the numbers and drive innovation forward.
